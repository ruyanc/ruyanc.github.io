AI for ENSO Forcasting

HUANG, T. W. and P. & HUANG, P. Superiority of a Convolutional Neural Network Model over Dynamical Models in Predicting Central Pacific ENSO. ADVANCES IN ATMOSPHERIC SCIENCES doi:10.1007/s00376-023-3001-1.

  • The study develops a convolutional neural network (CNN) model to predict sea surface temperature anomalies (SSTAs) in the tropical Pacific.
  • The CNN model is trained using historical simulations from 39 CMIP6 climate models.
  • The CNN model shows higher skill than dynamical models in predicting SSTAs in the equatorial western-central Pacific, especially for central Pacific (CP) El Nino events.
  • The CNN model captures small-scale precursors like the Pacific Meridional Mode that trigger CP El Nino 7 months in advance.
  • Dynamical models are better at predicting SSTAs in the eastern Pacific, where ocean dynamics dominates variability.
  • A fusion model combining the CNN and dynamical models achieves higher overall prediction skill for both CP and eastern Pacific ENSO events.
  • The integrated gradients method provides interpretability, revealing key small-scale precursors the CNN model uses for predicting CP events.
  • The study demonstrates the potential of combining deep learning and dynamical models to improve ENSO prediction, especially for CP events. The CNN model’s skill in identifying CP events from precursors could address a key limitation of current prediction systems.

Wang, H., Hu, S. & Li, X. An Interpretable Deep Learning ENSO Forecasting Model. Ocean-Land-Atmos. Res. 2, (2023).

Here is a summary of the key points from the PDF:

  • The study develops a deep learning model called STIEF to forecast the El Nino-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) using global ocean heat signatures.

  • The model uses 12 months of sea surface temperature (SST) and ocean heat content (OHC) anomaly maps as input.

  • Transfer learning is applied to overcome limited observational data, first training on CMIP simulations then fine-tuning on observations.

  • The model achieves an effective forecast length of 22 months, better than dynamical models, with minimal spring predictability barrier influence.

  • Diagnostics show ocean basin interactions and tropics-extratropics play a key role in ENSO development, confirmed by sensitivity experiments.

  • The model performance remains strong using SST alone, useful for operational forecasts lacking global subsurface data.

  • The study demonstrates a promising deep learning approach to extend ENSO predictability using the full spatiotemporal evolution of ocean heat signatures.
