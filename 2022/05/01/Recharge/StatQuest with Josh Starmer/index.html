Hypothesis Testing and The Null Hypothesis, Clearly Explained!!!

  • test the hypothesis by repeating the experiment
  • different: reject the hypothesis; otherwise, fail to reject the hypothesis
  • Null Hypothesis: no difference between things
  • the Null hypothesis does not require preliminary data

p-values: What they are and how to interpret them

  • the closer a p-value is to zero, the more confidence we have that A and B are different.
  • Getting a small p-value when there is no difference is called a Falise Positive
  • p = 0.05 means if there is no difference between A and B, 5% time we do the experiment, we’ll get a p-value less than 0.05
  • note: p-value does not tell how different A and B are

How to calculate p-values

  • Two-sided p-value is most common, one-sided is usually dangerous to use
